# Dubai Law No. (4) of 2022: Regulating Virtual Assets in the Emirate of Dubai

**Purpose**: Primary legislation establishing virtual asset regulatory framework in Dubai
**Key Assumptions**: Applies to all Dubai zones except DIFC; establishes VARA authority
**Usage Patterns**: Foundation for all VARA regulations and licensing requirements

---

## Overview

- **Issuer**: His Highness Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Ruler of Dubai
- **Effective Date**: Published in Official Gazette, February 28, 2022
- **Jurisdiction**: Emirate of Dubai (excluding DIFC)
- **Source**: https://rulebooks.vara.ae/rulebook/law-no-4-2022-regulating-virtual-assets-emirate-dubai

---

## Chapter One: Preliminary Provisions

### Article 1 - Definitions

Key terms defined:
- **Virtual Assets (VAs)**: Digital representations of value that can be digitally traded or transferred
- **Virtual Tokens**: Subset of virtual assets with specific characteristics
- **Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT)**: Technology enabling distributed data storage
- **Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP)**: Entity conducting VA activities

### Article 2 - Scope

- Applies throughout the Emirate of Dubai
- Excludes the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC)
- Covers all virtual asset activities within scope

---

## Chapter Two: VARA Regulatory Framework

### Article 3 - Establishment of VARA

- Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) established as public corporation
- Affiliated with Dubai World Trade Centre Authority
- Legal personality and independent financial status

### Article 4 - VARA Objectives

1. Promote Dubai as a global hub for virtual assets
2. Protect investors and market participants
3. Establish international standards for VA governance
4. Foster innovation while managing risks
5. Coordinate with federal and local authorities

### Article 5 - VARA Powers

- Licensing and registration of VASPs
- Supervision and monitoring of VA activities
- Issuance of regulations and guidelines
- Enforcement and disciplinary actions
- International cooperation

### Article 6 - Governance Structure

- Director General: Overall supervision
- CEO: Day-to-day operations management
- Board of Directors: Strategic oversight

---

## Chapter Three: Regulating Virtual Assets

### Article 7 - Regulated Activities

Seven categories requiring VARA permits:
1. Virtual asset platform operations
2. Virtual asset exchange services
3. Virtual asset transfer services
4. Virtual asset custody and wallet services
5. Virtual asset advisory services
6. Virtual asset management services
7. Virtual asset issuance services

### Article 8 - Licensing Requirements

- Permit required before conducting regulated activities
- Application process through VARA
- Fit and proper criteria for controllers

### Article 9 - VARA Regulatory Powers

- Suspend or revoke permits
- Restrict activities
- Impose conditions on licenses
- Issue binding directions

### Article 10 - Penalties

- Administrative fines for violations
- Suspension or revocation of permits
- Public censure
- Referral to criminal authorities where applicable

---

## Chapter Four: Final Provisions

### Article 11 - Liability Framework

- Civil and criminal liability for violations
- Directors and officers personal liability
- Whistleblower protections

### Article 12 - Grievance Procedures

- 30-day window for filing grievances
- Appeal mechanisms
- Administrative tribunal process

### Article 13 - Implementation

- VARA to issue implementing resolutions
- Transition period for existing operators
- Guidance published on VARA website
