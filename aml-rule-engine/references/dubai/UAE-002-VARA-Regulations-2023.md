# Virtual Assets and Related Activities Regulations 2023

## Introduction

The Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority ("VARA") was established and authorised by Law No. (4) of 2022 Regulating Virtual Assets in the Emirate of Dubai ("Dubai VA Law") to regulate Virtual Assets and Virtual Asset Service Providers ("VASPs").

VARA is affiliated to the Dubai World Trade Centre Authority ("DWTCA") and is the competent entity in charge of regulating, supervising, and overseeing Virtual Assets and VA Activities in all zones across the Emirate of Dubai, including Special Development Zones and Free Zones but excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre ("Emirate").

### VARA Objectives (Article 5, Dubai VA Law)

1. Promote the Emirate as a regional and international hub for Virtual Assets and related services
2. Increase awareness on investment in the Virtual Asset services and products sector
3. Contribute to attracting investments and encourage VA companies to base their business in the Emirate
4. Develop regulations for investor protection and curbing illegal practices
5. Develop regulations, rules and standards for regulating, supervising and overseeing VA platforms

---

## Fundamental Principles and Goals

### Regulatory Principles

1. **Market integrity and stability**: Fair, orderly, transparent markets; prevention of fraud and criminal activity; FATF compliant regime
2. **Consumer protection**: Prevent harms from misinformation, abuse and poor operational practices; require 'informed consent'
3. **Technology neutrality and innovation**: Regulate activities not technology; not ban legitimate activities
4. **Regulatory resilience**: Principles-driven approach that doesn't become quickly outdated
5. **Regulatory efficiency and proportionality**: Effective and least burdensome approach; justified relative to potential harm

### Primary Policy Goals

1. Promote the Emirate and UAE as a safe and progressive jurisdiction for Virtual Asset growth and innovation
2. Position VARA as trusted and respected internationally, enabling FATF principles and establishing convergence point

## Part I - The Regulator

### A. Establishment of VARA

1. **VARA Jurisdiction**: The Dubai VA Law and Regulations apply to all Virtual Assets and VA Activities in the Emirate
2. **VARA Powers**: VARA has sole and absolute discretion to interpret, waive, modify or otherwise adapt these Regulations
3. **Power to Amend**: VARA may amend these Regulations at any time

### B. Rules, Directives and Guidance

1. **Rules**: Binding effect; contained in Regulations, Rulebooks or other Rule-making instruments; published on website
2. **Directives**: May direct any Entity or VASP to take or refrain from specific actions; communicated in writing
3. **Guidance**: Indicative and non-binding; elaboration or explanation on application of Regulations, Rules or Directives

---

## Part II - Issuing Virtual Assets

### A. Issuance Rules
Any Entity in the Emirate issuing a Virtual Asset in the course of business must comply with the VA Issuance Rulebook

### B. Power to Classify Virtual Assets
VARA may classify any Virtual Asset as being:
- Prohibited in the Emirate
- Regulated by CBUAE
- Subject to specific regulatory treatment

### C. Prohibited Virtual Assets
**The issuance of Anonymity-Enhanced Cryptocurrencies and all VA Activities related to them are prohibited in the Emirate.**

---

## Part III - Regulated VA Activities

### A. General Prohibition and Exemptions

1. **General Prohibition**: No Entity may carry out any VA Activity by way of business unless:
   - Authorised and Licensed by VARA
   - An employee on behalf of a Licensed employer
   - An Exempt Entity

2. **"By way of business" Determination**: VARA considers:
   - Whether Entity holds itself out as conducting VA Activity
   - Regularity, scale and continuity of activity
   - Commercial element (remuneration, benefits)

3. **False Claims Prohibited**: Non-licensed Entities must not claim to be VASPs or Exempt Entities

4. **UAE Central Bank**: UAE-CBDCs remain under exclusive CBUAE regulatory purview

---

## Part IV - Licensing

### A. Licensing Requirements

1. **Requirement to Hold a Licence**: All Entities wishing to carry out VA Activities must seek authorisation and obtain a Licence from VARA

2. **Licensing Conditions**: VASPs must comply with all licensing conditions including all Regulations, Rules and Directives

3. **Activities Outside the Emirate**: VASPs must comply with all Regulations as minimum standard; meet higher of two regulatory standards

4. **Professional Exemption**: Applies to registered lawyers, accountants and licensed business consultants carrying out VA Activity wholly incidental to professional practice

5. **Exempt Entities**: Must notify VARA and obtain no-objection confirmation; comply with all requirements

6. **Mandatory Registration for Large Proprietary Traders**: Entities investing own portfolio >= USD 250,000,000 in VAs during any rolling 30 days must register with VARA

7. **Voluntary Registration**: Available for DLT technology service providers and proprietary VA investors

8. **Critical Service Providers**: VARA may designate certain service providers as critical or systemically important

### B. VARA's Licensing and Authorisation Powers

1. **Granting of Licences**: VARA may grant, vary, suspend or revoke Licences in its discretion

2. **Suspension/Revocation Grounds**:
   - Material violation of laws, Regulations, Rules or Directives
   - Good Cause
   - Insolvency
   - Failure to pay court judgments

3. **Authorisation and Supervision Fees**: As set out in Schedule 2

## Part VI - Anti-Money Laundering and Combating the Financing of Terrorism

### A. VARA's Supervisory Authority

1. Made in recognition of Federal Decree-Law No. (20) of 2018 on AML and CFT ("Federal AML-CFT Laws")
2. Reference to money laundering includes financing of terrorism and all other unlawful organisations, and sanctions non-compliance
3. VARA is designated as Supervisory Authority for the Emirate in respect of all VASPs and VA Activities
4. VARA has power to supervise compliance with Federal AML-CFT Laws
5. VARA shall report suspicious conduct to relevant authorities

### B. AML/CFT Obligations of VASPs

VASPs must comply with all:
- Federal AML-CFT Laws
- All other legislation and regulatory requirements
- These Regulations, Rules and Directives
- FATF recommendations as incorporated by VARA

### A. Marketing Regulations

## Part VIII - Market Offences

### A. Market Offences

The following are Market Offences:
1. **Insider Dealing**
2. **Unlawful Disclosure**
3. **Market Manipulation**

### B. Inside Information

Inside Information means information of a precise nature, which has not been made public, and which if made public would reasonably be expected to affect the price of a Virtual Asset.

### C-E. Insider Dealing and Unlawful Disclosure

Prohibition on insider dealing and unlawful disclosure of inside information.

### F-G. Legitimate Behaviour and Market Sounding

Exemptions for legitimate behaviour and proper market sounding procedures.

### H-I. Market Manipulation

Prohibition on market manipulation including:
- False or misleading transactions
- Price manipulation
- Wash trading
- Spoofing
- Layering

### J. Prevention and Detection

VASPs must have systems and controls to prevent and detect market offences.

---

## Part IX - Supervision, Examination and Enforcement

### A. Investigation or Examination by VARA

VARA may investigate or examine any VASP or Entity at any time.

### B. Examination

VARA has full power to examine VASPs' books, records, systems and operations.

### C. Enforcement, Fines and Penalties

VARA may impose fines and penalties as set out in Schedule 3.

## Part X - Confidentiality
