# Part III - Anti-Money Laundering and Combating the Financing of Terrorism

## Introduction

Part III of this Compliance and Risk Management Rulebook sets out requirements which aim to prevent the use of Virtual Assets and services relating to them in furtherance of illicit activities. VARA considers such illicit activities to include money laundering and the financing of terrorism, as well as proliferation financing and sanctions non-compliance.

---

## A. Appointment and Duties of Money Laundering Reporting Officer

### A.1 MLRO Requirements

VASPs shall appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer who:
- Possesses at least two (2) years of experience handling AML/CFT matters
- Is a Fit and Proper Person ("MLRO")

Such appointment shall be reviewed annually to ensure that the MLRO remains a Fit and Proper Person capable of discharging all relevant duties.

### A.2 MLRO Responsibilities

The MLRO shall be responsible for:

1. Ensuring the Board and Staff are properly and adequately trained in respect of their understanding and compliance with all applicable AML/CFT laws and regulatory requirements

2. Developing and implementing AML/CFT policies and procedures

3. Conducting AML/CFT risk assessments and implementing necessary changes to relevant policies and procedures

4. Monitoring and reporting Suspicious Transactions

5. Ensuring appropriate corrective actions are taken in response to non-compliance with Federal AML-CFT Laws

6. Reporting to the Board on a quarterly basis on the effectiveness of the VASP's AML/CFT policies and procedures

7. Ensuring quarterly reports include a summary of all Anonymity-Enhanced Transactions and clients involved

8. Making reports available to VARA on request

### A.3 Delegation

AML/CFT activities may be delegated to appropriate Entities, provided that:
- The MLRO continues to be held accountable
- All applicable requirements in the Company Rulebook are complied with

### A.4 Multiple Roles

The MLRO may hold more than one non-client facing role within the VASP, provided such roles do not create conflicting duties (including CO and head of risk function).

## B. Policies and Procedures

### B.1 Compliance Requirements

VASPs will establish and implement policies and procedures to comply with all AML/CFT requirements including:

1. Federal AML-CFT Laws
2. FATF's 12-Month Review of VA/VASP Standards (June 2020)
3. FATF's Second 12-Month Review (July 2021)
4. FATF's Updated Guidance for RBA to VAs and VASPs (October 2021)
5. FATF 40 Recommendations (March 2022)
6. UAE EOCN Guidance on Counter Proliferation Financing (March 2022)
7. EOCN's Local Terrorist List (as amended)

### B.2 Required Courses of Action

Policies and procedures must establish courses of action allowing VASPs to:

1. Refrain from opening or conducting transactions under anonymous or fictitious names
2. Promptly apply directives from UAE authorities for implementing UNSC Resolutions
3. Maintain all records for transactions (local and international)
4. Ensure full compliance with applicable AML/CFT requirements

### B.3 Risk Screening Rules

VASPs shall establish adequate risk rules to screen:
- Clients and UBOs
- Virtual Asset transactions
- VA Wallet addresses

To identify:
- Potential illicit activities
- Adverse information in higher risk situations
- Applicability of targeted financial sanctions

### B.4 Attestation

All policies and procedures must be:
- Attested by a competent third party
- Submitted to VARA in the licensing process
- Submitted within 21 calendar days after any changes

## C. AML/CFT Controls

### C.1 Effective Controls

VASPs should have effective AML/CFT controls and systems including:
- Distributed ledger analytics tools
- Investigative tools for monitoring and screening transactions

### C.2 Analytics Tools Review

VASPs should review and document:
- Capabilities and weaknesses of distributed ledger analytics tools
- Design controls to monitor clients' interaction with VA Activities

### C.3 Performance Review

VASPs should review and document the performance and function of any distributed ledger analytics tools used for ongoing monitoring.

### C.4 Red Flag Indicators

VASPs shall implement internal controls to address the FATF Report "Virtual Assets Red Flags Indicators of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing" (September 2020) when designing transaction monitoring scenarios and thresholds.

## D. Risk Assessments

### D.1 Business Risk Assessment

VASPs must conduct AML/CFT business risk assessments to:
- Understand, identify and assess ML risks specific to their business
- Consider nature, size, and complexity

### D.2 Risk Areas to Assess

Including but not limited to:
1. Virtual Assets (especially Anonymity-Enhanced Cryptocurrencies)
2. VA-related technologies, products or services
3. Business and professional practices
4. Other technologies (e.g., AI and machine learning)
5. Other emerging risks

### D.3 Assessment Frequency

Business risk assessments required:
- At regular intervals no longer than every **3 months**
- Upon significant change in any risk area

### D.4 Assessment Outcomes

VASPs must demonstrate that outcomes directly inform:
- Development and update of AML/CFT policies
- Allocation of resources in AML/CFT activities

### D.5 Anonymity-Enhanced Transactions

VASPs enabling Anonymity-Enhanced Transactions must implement proportionately enhanced controls including:
- Enhanced CDD on each client
- CDD verification every **6 months**

### D.6-D.8 Client Risk Assessment

VASPs must conduct client risk assessments including:
- Criteria and methodology for categorisation
- Documentation of risk tiers and associated actions
- Comprehensive audit trails

Client risk assessments required at regular intervals no longer than every **3 months**.

## E. Client Due Diligence (CDD)

### E.1 Risk-Based Approach

VASPs shall adopt a risk-based application of CDD measures in accordance with Federal AML-CFT Laws.

### E.2 Risk Rating

VASPs shall undertake risk-based assessment of every client and assign a risk rating proportionate to assessed AML/CFT risks.

### E.3 Timing of CDD

CDD measures required before or during:
- Establishment of business relationship
- Execution of transaction for non-account holders

### E.4 When CDD is Required

1. Establishing business relationship for VA Activities
2. Occasional transactions **>= AED 3,500** (single or linked)
3. Potential Suspicious Transaction
4. Doubts about previously obtained information
5. Any transaction for high-risk clients

### E.5 Ongoing Supervision

Including:
- Auditing transactions throughout business relationship
- Ensuring CDD records are up-to-date

### E.6 Verification Documents

**For Individuals:**
- Full name (with copy of ID/travel document)
- Nationality
- Address
- Place of birth
- Name and address of employer
- PEP approval from MLRO and Senior Management

**For Entities:**
- Full name
- Type of Entity
- Constitutional documents (attested by UAE authorities)
- Principal place of business
- Names of Senior Management
- PEP approval if applicable

### E.7 Additional Requirements

VASPs must:
- Verify authorization of representatives
- Understand intended purpose of business relationship
- Understand client's business, ownership and control structure
- Identify UBOs
- Identify any DAOs and their purpose

### E.8 Inability to Conduct CDD

If unable to conduct appropriate CDD, VASPs shall NOT:
- Establish or maintain business relationship
- Execute any transaction

### E.9 Third Party Reliance

VASPs remain liable when relying on third parties for CDD.

### E.10 Enhanced CDD

Required for high-risk clients or PEP UBOs:

1. Obtain additional identification information
2. Obtain information on intended nature of business
3. Obtain reasons for transactions
4. Update CDD more regularly
5. Identify and verify source of funds and wealth
6. Carry out enhanced monitoring
7. Obtain Senior Management approval
8. Require first transaction through licensed institution
9. For natural persons: verify current residential address

## F. Suspicious Transaction Monitoring and Reporting

### F.1 Monitoring Methods

VASPs shall employ appropriate methods to:
- Continuously monitor business relationships
- Identify Suspicious Transactions
- Ensure no 'tipping-off' occurs
- Immediately report to MLRO

### F.2 Indicators

VASPs shall put in place and regularly update indicators to identify possible Suspicious Transactions.

### F.3 MLRO Reporting Duties

Upon suspicion of crime-related proceeds, the MLRO shall:
1. Immediately report to UAE FIU
2. Respond to additional information requests within **48 hours**
3. Undertake additional actions within specified timeframes
4. Report to CO that STR has been made (if not same individual)

### F.4 AML-CFT Reports

Reports shall be made:
- To UAE FIU on GoAML platform
- In required form and content
- In accordance with VARA Guidance

### F.5 Ongoing Monitoring

VASPs shall continue monitoring (near real-time where appropriate) transactions subject to AML-CFT Reports.

## G. FATF Travel Rule

### G.1 Compliance Requirement

VASPs must comply with all Federal AML-CFT Laws including Travel Rule requirements.

### G.2 Originator Requirements

Prior to initiating any transfer **> AED 3,500**, VASPs must obtain and hold:
- Required and accurate originator information
- Required beneficiary information

### G.3 Beneficiary VASP Requirements

Prior to permitting access to received Virtual Assets **> AED 3,500**, must obtain and hold:
- Required originator information
- Required and accurate beneficiary information

### G.4 Minimum Originator Information

- Name
- Account number or VA Wallet address
- Residential or business address

### G.5 Minimum Beneficiary Information

- Name
- Account number or VA Wallet address

### G.6 Counterparty Due Diligence

VASPs must complete risk-based due diligence on counterparty VASPs before any transaction.

### G.7 Risk Handling

VASPs must consider risks associated with:
- Deposits or withdrawals (compliant and non-compliant)
- Non-obliged entities (unhosted VA Wallets)
- Anonymity-Enhanced Transactions

### G.8 Demonstration to VARA

VASPs must demonstrate Travel Rule compliance during licensing and submit relevant policies.

### G.9 Implementation Guidance

VASPs shall be guided by FATF Interpretive Note to Recommendation 15.

### G.10 Reporting

VARA may require VASPs to report on Travel Rule compliance at any time.

## H. Compliance with Targeted Financial Sanctions

### H.1 Screening Requirements

VASPs must screen all clients and transactions against:
- UNSC sanctions frameworks
- Federal AML-CFT Laws designated lists

### H.2 Automated Screening

VASPs must:
- Implement automated screening systems
- Update systems regularly with latest sanctions lists
- Operate in real-time

### H.3 Freezing Requirements

VASPs must:
- Take immediate action to freeze assets of designated Entities
- Ensure no withdrawals, transfers, or use during freezing period

### H.4 Freezing Policies

VASPs must establish policies to:
- Immediately freeze upon detecting a match
- Maintain records for minimum **8 years**

### H.5 Transaction Prohibition

VASPs must block and prohibit:
- Any transactions involving designated entities
- Attempts to bypass sanctions

---

## I. Record Keeping

### I.1 Record Types

VASPs shall retain:
1. VA transaction records (including on public distributed ledgers)
2. CDD records
3. Third party CDD information
4. Ongoing monitoring records
5. Suspicious Transaction reports

### I.2 Retention Period

All records must be retained for minimum **8 years**.

---

## J. Enforcement

### J.1 Non-Compliance

VASPs failing to comply with Part III may be subject to:
- Enforcement actions by VARA
- Penalties under Regulations and Federal AML-CFT Laws

### J.2 Individuals Subject to Enforcement

Enforcement may be taken against:
- The VASP
- Directors
- Responsible Individuals
- MLRO
- Senior Management
