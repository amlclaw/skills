# D. Risk Management

## Core Requirements

1. VASPs shall establish and maintain:
   a. an effective risk management function;
   b. policies and procedures; and
   c. risk measurement and reporting methodologies,

   Each commensurate with the nature, size, complexity, and risk profile of the VASP in order to identify, measure, quantify, manage and monitor the risks, whether financial, technological or otherwise, to which they are or may be exposed.

2. The risk management function should consist of a sufficient number of suitably qualified and experienced Staff. The head of the risk function must have the appropriate qualifications and authority to oversee and monitor the overall risk exposures of the VASP.

3. The Board shall ensure that the risk management policies are subject to ongoing comprehensive review, particularly when there is a material change in the VASP's business, operations or Senior Management or Staff.

4. The head of the risk function shall submit risk exposure reports to the Board at least once every quarter.

## Key Risk Categories

### a. Financial Stability Risks
- **Financial soundness**: Risks when VASP lacks necessary capital, liquidity or reserves
- **Market risk**: Risks from changing market conditions
- **Credit risks**: Risks from credit exposure to clients and counterparties
- **Liquidity risks**: Risks from asset and liability mix

### b. Market Conduct Risks
- Business strategy risks
- Client onboarding risks
- Organisation and regulation risks
- Operational risks
- Quality of management & corporate governance
- Relationship with regulators
- Cybersecurity risks

### c. Compliance and Risk Management Risks
- AML/CFT, market abuse & fraud risks
- Outsourcing & counterparty risks
- Risk management systems risks
- Compliance function and arrangements
- Business continuity risks

### d. Consumer Protection Risks
- Communications with clients & financial promotions
- Legal risks
- Disclosure and reporting
- Client assets risks
