## Introduction

Part III of the Compliance and Risk Management Rulebook sets out requirements which aim to prevent the use of Virtual Assets and services relating to them in furtherance of illicit activities. VARA considers such illicit activities to include money laundering and the financing of terrorism, as well as proliferation financing and sanctions non-compliance.

---

## A. Appointment and Duties of Money Laundering Reporting Officer

1. VASPs shall appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) who:
   - possesses at least two (2) years of experience handling AML/CFT matters; and
   - is a Fit and Proper Person

2. The MLRO shall be responsible for:
   - ensuring the Board and Staff are properly and adequately trained
   - developing and implementing AML/CFT policies and procedures
   - conducting AML/CFT risk assessments
   - monitoring and reporting Suspicious Transactions
   - ensuring appropriate corrective actions are taken
   - reporting to the Board on a quarterly basis
   - ensuring quarterly reports include a summary of all Anonymity-Enhanced Transactions

---

## B. Policies and Procedures

VASPs will establish and implement policies and procedures to comply with all AML/CFT requirements including:
- Federal AML-CFT Laws
- FATF 12-Month Review (June 2020)
- FATF Second 12-Month Review (July 2021)
- FATF Updated Guidance for RBA to VA and VASPs (October 2021)
- FATF Recommendations (March 2022)
- UAE EOCN Guidance on CPF (March 2022)
- EOCN's Local Terrorist List

---

## C. AML/CFT Controls

1. VASPs should have effective AML/CFT controls and systems including distributed ledger analytics tools
2. VASPs should review and document the capabilities and weaknesses of such tools
3. Information about VA transactions and VA Wallet addresses are dynamic in nature
4. VASPs shall implement internal controls to address the FATF Report Virtual Assets Red Flags Indicators

---

## D. Risk Assessments

### Business Risk Assessment
- Must be conducted at regular intervals no longer than every three (3) months
- Must consider new or existing VAs, technologies, products, services, business practices

### Client Risk Assessment
- Must categorize each client's AML/CFT risk
- Must document associated courses of action
- Must maintain comprehensive audit trails

---

## E. Client Due Diligence (CDD)

### Standard CDD
Required when:
- Establishing a business relationship
- Carrying out occasional transactions >= AED 3,500
- Handling potential Suspicious Transactions
- Doubts about previously obtained identification
- High-risk client transactions

### For Individuals:
- Full name, nationality, address, place of birth
- Name and address of employer
- PEP status check

### For Entities:
- Full name and type of Entity
- Constitutional documents
- Principal place of business
- Names of Senior Management
- UBO identification

### Enhanced CDD (High-Risk)
- Additional identification information
- Information on intended nature of business relationship
- Source of funds and wealth verification
- Enhanced monitoring
- Senior Management approval

---

## F. Suspicious Transaction Monitoring and Reporting

1. VASPs shall employ appropriate methods to continuously monitor business relationships
2. VASPs shall put in place and regularly update indicators for Suspicious Transactions
3. Upon suspicion, MLRO shall:
   - Immediately report to UAE FIU
   - Respond to additional information requests within 48 hours
   - Undertake additional actions as requested
4. AML-CFT Reports shall be made via GoAML platform

---

## G. FATF Travel Rule

1. Prior to initiating any transfer >= AED 3,500, VASPs must obtain originator and beneficiary information
2. Required originator information: name, account number/wallet address, residential/business address
3. Required beneficiary information: name, account number/wallet address
4. Risk-based due diligence on counterparty VASPs required
5. Must address risks associated with unhosted wallets and Anonymity-Enhanced Transactions

---

## H. Compliance with Targeted Financial Sanctions

### Screening
- Screen all clients and transactions against UNSC sanctions frameworks and Federal AML-CFT Laws
- Implement automated screening systems updated regularly

### Freezing
- Take immediate action to freeze assets associated with designated Entities
- Maintain records of all freezing actions for minimum 8 years

### Prohibition
- Block and prohibit transactions involving designated Entities

---

## I. Record Keeping

VASPs shall retain the following for no less than 8 years:
- Virtual Asset transaction records
- CDD records
- Information relating to third parties engaged for CDD
- Records relating to ongoing monitoring
- Suspicious Transaction reports

---

## J. Enforcement

VASPs which fail to comply may be subject to enforcement actions by VARA or penalties under the Regulations and Federal AML-CFT Laws. Enforcement may be taken against the VASP, its directors, Responsible Individuals, MLRO and/or Senior Management.
