# Regulations on the Marketing of Virtual Assets and Related Activities 2024

## Introduction

VARA is affiliated to the Dubai World Trade Centre Authority (DWTCA), and is the competent entity in charge of regulating, supervising, and overseeing Virtual Assets and VA Activities in all zones across the Emirate of Dubai, including Special Development Zones and Free Zones, but excluding the Dubai International Financial Centre (the Emirate).

As set out in Article 5 of the Dubai VA Law, VARA has the following objectives:
1. To promote the Emirate as a regional and international hub for Virtual Assets and related services; to boost the competitive edge of the Emirate at the local and international levels; and to develop the digital economy in the Emirate
2. To increase awareness on investment in the Virtual Asset services and products sector, and encourage innovation in this sector
3. To contribute to attracting investments and encourage companies operating in the field of Virtual Assets to base their business in the Emirate
4. To develop the regulations required for the protection of investors and dealers in Virtual Assets, and to endeavour to curb illegal practices in coordination with the concerned entities
5. To develop the regulations, rules and standards required for regulating, supervising, and overseeing Virtual Asset platforms, VASPs, and all other matters related to Virtual Assets

These Marketing Regulations apply to all Marketing of or relating to Virtual Assets or VA Activities in or targeting the UAE. Market participants should note that requirements in these Marketing Regulations are applicable to all Entities, including domestic and/or foreign Entities, and whether they are authorised and Licensed by VARA to carry out VA Activities or not.

## Part I - Marketing of Virtual Assets and Related Activities

### A. Marketing in the UAE

1. "Marketing" means any advertisement, invitation, inducement, solicitation, offer or promotion.

2. Any advertisement, invitation, inducement, solicitation, offer or promotion may qualify as Marketing regardless of the media or channel through which it is made, including but not limited to:
   - Communications, publications, dissemination of any form of data, information, or content that is promotional, influenced and/or sponsored material
   - Social media posts, blogs, comments, endorsements, non-written communications, banners, billboards, videos, podcasts, recordings or live streams
   - Events held in the Emirate which may facilitate the solicitation of clients or incentivise the use of any product and/or service related to any VA Activity
   - Advertisements, sponsored editorials, paid or earned media, and all forms of publicity-driving content or materials including branding and merchandise
   - "Airdrops" or the issuance, giving or transfer of a Virtual Asset
   - Educational content including articles, papers, presentations, discussions and tutorials

### B. General Prohibitions

1. All Marketing of or relating to any Virtual Asset or VA Activity in or targeting the UAE must comply with these Marketing Regulations.

2. No Entity may carry out any Marketing of or relating to any Virtual Asset or VA Activity in or targeting the UAE, unless it complies with these Marketing Regulations at all times.

3. **Marketing of VA Activities**: All Marketing of or relating to any VA Activity in or targeting the UAE, must only be carried out:
   - By a VASP Licensed by VARA to carry out the VA Activity to which the Marketing relates; or
   - On behalf of, and approved by, a VASP Licensed by VARA to carry out the VA Activity to which the Marketing relates

4. **Anonymity-Enhanced Cryptocurrencies**: The Marketing of any Anonymity-Enhanced Cryptocurrencies in or targeting the UAE, and any VA Activity(ies) involving Anonymity-Enhanced Cryptocurrencies is strictly prohibited in the Emirate.

### C. Requirements for Marketing

1. **Compliance with applicable laws**: All Marketing must comply with:
   - All applicable laws, regulations, guidelines or other rules in the UAE and the Emirate
   - All requirements to obtain and maintain necessary approvals, permits and/or licences
   - Any other conditions as may be communicated by VARA

2. **General Requirements**: All Marketing must:
   - Be fair, clear and not misleading in both substance and presentation
   - Be clearly identifiable as marketing or promotional in nature
   - Not contain content inconsistent with the following:
     - Virtual Assets may lose their value in part or in full, and are subject to extreme volatility
     - Virtual Assets may not always be transferable, and some transfers may be irreversible
     - The owner/investor can lose all or part of money invested and will not benefit from financial protection
     - Virtual Assets may not be liquid
     - Virtual Assets are not all private and may be recorded on public DLTs
     - Virtual Assets may be subject to fraud, manipulation, theft
   - Not contain inaccurate or misleading information
   - Not state or imply that investments are safe, low risk or that returns are guaranteed
   - Not state or imply that investment decisions are trivial, simple, or easy
   - Not state or imply that past performance guarantees future results
   - Not state or imply urgency or create fear of missing out
   - Not promote acquisition of Virtual Assets using credit

3. **Marketing of Virtual Assets** must:
   - Not include a call to buy, or messaging seeking the purchase/sale of the Virtual Asset
   - Include a prominent disclaimer that Virtual Assets may lose their value in full or in part
   - State clearly that the owner/investor can lose all money invested and does not benefit from financial protection
   - Not involve sending any Virtual Asset to a VA Wallet without prior consent

4. **Record Keeping**: All Entities Marketing any VA Activity or Virtual Asset must retain records for a minimum of eight (8) years.

### D. Exemptions

1. **Journalistic Exemption**: Applies to content posted by licensed journalists where:
   - The overall purpose is not Marketing
   - Content includes prominent disclaimer explaining interests
   - If content references purchase of VA, includes volatility disclaimer

2. **Educational Exemption**: Applies to educational content where:
   - The overall purpose is not Marketing
   - Content includes disclaimer explaining interests

3. **Personal or Private Communications Exemption**: Purely personal or private communications are not considered Marketing.

4. **Key Opinion Leaders**: Are NOT deemed to qualify under journalistic or educational exemptions and must comply with Marketing Regulations in full.

### E. Platforms and Channels

1. All Entities that facilitate Marketing must take commercially reasonable steps to ensure compliance with all applicable laws.

2. **Records**: Must maintain records of due diligence for eight (8) years.

3. **Application Stores**: Operators must ensure any app facilitating VA Activity is either:
   - Owned/controlled by a VARA Licensed VASP; or
   - Otherwise approved by VARA

### F. Events in the Emirate

1. **Marketing at Events**: By limited exemption, unlicensed Entities may carry out Marketing at physical events provided they:
   - Do not carry out VA Activity in the Emirate unless Licensed
   - Do not permit UAE residents to sign-up at the event
   - All Marketing includes disclaimer that they are not Licensed by VARA
   - Marketing consists only of: Entity name, logo, reference to activities, and educational information

2. **Event Organisers**: Must:
   - Ensure all attendees are suitable/qualified
   - Maintain list of all attendees
   - Obtain signed undertaking from exhibitors
   - Suspend or cancel event if notified by VARA to do so

### G. Marketing from the Emirate in Other Jurisdictions

All Entities in the Emirate must comply with applicable laws in any jurisdiction outside of the Emirate when carrying out Marketing in such jurisdiction.

## Part II - Supervision, Examination and Enforcement

### A. Supervision, Examination and Enforcement

VARA has full discretionary power to supervise, examine and enforce compliance with these Marketing Regulations.

|-----|---------------------|-------------------|

| 4 | Third party Marketing violations | Up to 2,000,000 |
| 5 | Journalistic exemption violations | Up to 2,000,000 |
| 6 | Educational exemption violations | Up to 2,000,000 |
| 7 | Platform/channel facilitation violations | Up to 10,000,000 |
| 8 | Platform record keeping violations | Up to 500,000 |
| 9 | Application store violations | Up to 2,000,000 |
| 10 | Event Marketing violations | Up to 10,000,000 |
| 11 | Event organiser violations | Up to 10,000,000 |
| 12 | Marketing from Emirate violations | Up to 10,000,000 |
| 13 | Other violations | Up to 10,000,000 |
| 14 | Failure to comply with enforcement actions | Up to 10,000,000 |
| 15 | Repeat violation within 1 year | Double the applicable fine |
| 16 | Non-payment of fine | 1% per month compounding |
