# Hong Kong Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance (Cap. 615)

**Purpose**: Primary AML/CFT legislation establishing VASP licensing regime in Hong Kong
**Key Assumptions**: Applies to all VATPs operating in or targeting Hong Kong; excludes DIFC
**Usage Patterns**: VASP license verification; AML/CFT compliance requirements; penalty framework

---

## Overview

- **Legislation**: Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance (Cap. 615)
- **VASP Regime Effective**: 1 June 2023
- **Authority**: Securities and Futures Commission (SFC)
- **Source**: https://www.elegislation.gov.hk/hk/cap615

---

## Part 1 - Licensing Requirements

### Organizational Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Incorporation** | Hong Kong incorporated company or registered overseas entity |
| **Minimum Paid-up Capital** | HK$5,000,000 |
| **Minimum Liquid Capital** | HK$3,000,000 or SFO s.2 basic amount (whichever higher) |
| **Liquid Assets** | Cash, deposits, treasury bills, CDs equivalent to 12 months operating expenses |
| **Asset Location** | Must be maintained in Hong Kong |

### Personnel & Governance

- Minimum two officers to directly supervise relevant activities
- At least one responsible officer must ordinarily reside in Hong Kong
- Senior management bears primary responsibility for conduct standards
- Two classes of designated personnel: responsible officers and licensed representatives

### Fit and Proper Test

SFC evaluates:
1. Financial status or solvency
2. Educational qualifications and experience
3. Ability to operate competently, honestly, and fairly
4. Character and financial integrity
5. Reputation and reliability

## Part 2 - Regulated Activities

### VA Service Definition

Operations providing electronic facilities where:
1. Buy/sell offers for VAs occur regularly forming binding transactions
2. Persons are introduced to negotiate VA purchases/sales
3. Client money or VAs come into direct/indirect possession of provider

### Scope of Application

- Applies to VATPs serving Hong Kong residents
- Covers both domestic and overseas operators targeting HK
- Non-security tokens require minimum 12 months track record for listing eligibility
- Initial restriction: may only service professional investors

### Exemptions

- VA exchanges already regulated under SFC opt-in regime (offering security tokens)

## Part 3 - AML/CFT Obligations

Licensed VASPs classified as "financial institutions" under AMLO, requiring:

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

- Client onboarding and suitability assessments
- Risk tolerance evaluation
- Risk comprehension verification
- Identity verification before establishing business relationship

### Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Required for:
- High-risk customers
- Politically exposed persons (PEPs)
- Complex or unusual transactions
- Cross-border correspondent relationships

### Record Keeping

- Minimum 6-year retention for transaction records
- Customer identification records
- Account files and business correspondence

### Sanctions Compliance

Compliance required under:
- United Nations (Anti-Terrorism Measures) Ordinance (UNATMO)
- United Nations Sanctions Ordinance (UNSO)
- Weapons of Mass Destruction Ordinance (WMD)

### Suspicious Transaction Reporting

- Report to Joint Financial Intelligence Unit (JFIU)
- No tipping-off to customers
- Internal escalation procedures required

---

## Part 4 - Travel Rule Requirements

### Information Requirements for VA Transfers

**Originator Information**:
- Name
- Account number (or unique transaction reference)
- Address, national identity number, or date/place of birth

**Beneficiary Information**:
- Name
- Account number (or unique transaction reference)

### Threshold

- Full information required for transfers >= HK$8,000
- Simplified requirements for transfers < HK$8,000

## Part 5 - Penalties and Enforcement

### Operating Without License

| Offense | Fine | Imprisonment |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Unlicensed operation | HK$5,000,000 | Up to 7 years |

### License Application Offenses

| Offense | Fine | Imprisonment |
|---------|------|--------------|
| False/misleading statements | HK$1,000,000 | Up to 2 years |

### Non-Compliance Penalties

| Offense | Fine | Imprisonment |
|---------|------|--------------|
| VASP non-compliance | HK$1,000,000 | Up to 2 years |
| Responsible officer non-compliance | HK$1,000,000 | Up to 2 years |

### Fraud Offenses

| Offense | Fine | Imprisonment |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Fraud (s53ZRF) | HK$10,000,000 | Up to 10 years |
| Fraudulent misrepresentation (s53ZRG) | HK$10,000,000 | Up to 7 years |

## Part 6 - Transitional Arrangements

### Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1 January 2023 | General provisions effective |
| 1 March 2023 | Activity restrictions; pre-existing operator transitions |
| 1 June 2023 | VASP licensing regime fully effective |
| 1 October 2023 | Deadline for existing operators to apply |
| 1 June 2024 | End of non-contravention period |

### Deemed Licensed Status

Pre-existing VA exchange operators who:
1. File SFC application within 9 months of regime commencement
2. Confirm regulatory compliance

Receive "deemed licensed" status until SFC decides application.
