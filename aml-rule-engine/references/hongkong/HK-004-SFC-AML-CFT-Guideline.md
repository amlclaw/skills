# Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing Guideline

Issued by the Securities and Futures Commission for Associated Entities of Licensed Corporations and SFC-licensed Virtual Asset Service Providers

## Legal Basis

This Guideline is published under:
- Section 399 of the Securities and Futures Ordinance, Cap. 571 (SFO)
- Section 53ZTK of the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance, Cap. 615 (AMLO)

## Purpose

The Guideline on Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Financing of Terrorism (For Licensed Corporations and SFC-licensed VAS Providers) sets out:
- Relevant AML/CFT statutory and regulatory requirements
- AML/CFT standards for Licensed Corporations (LCs) and SFC-licensed Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs)

## Scope

### Applies to:
- Associated Entities (AEs) that are not authorized financial institutions
- Licensed Corporations
- SFC-licensed Virtual Asset Service Providers

### Content Coverage:
- General background on money laundering and terrorist financing (ML/TF)
- Summary of main provisions of applicable AML/CFT legislation in Hong Kong
- Practical guidance for designing and implementing policies, procedures and controls

## Compliance Requirements

### For Associated Entities (AEs)
- AEs are expected to have regard to the provisions of the Guideline for LCs and SFC-licensed VAS Providers as if they were themselves LCs and/or SFC-licensed VAS Providers

### For Authorized Financial Institutions
Should have regard to:
- HKMA's Guideline on Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Financing of Terrorism
- Paragraph 4.1.6 for definition of "customer" for securities sector
- Paragraph 4.20 for cross-border correspondent relationships
- Chapter 12 for provisions relating to virtual assets
- Appendix B for indicators of suspicious transactions in securities sector

## Mandatory Requirements

The use of "must" or "should" in this Guideline indicates a **mandatory requirement**.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

- Failure to comply does not by itself render the person liable to judicial proceedings
- In proceedings under AMLO or SFO, this Guideline is admissible in evidence
- Failure may reflect adversely on fitness and properness
