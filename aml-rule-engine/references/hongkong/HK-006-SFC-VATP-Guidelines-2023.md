## I. Interpretation and Application

### 1.1 Definitions

**"Associated Entity"** means a company which:
- (i) has notified the SFC that it has become an "associated entity" of a Platform Operator under section 165 of the SFO and/or section 53ZRW of the AMLO
- (ii) is incorporated in Hong Kong
- (iii) holds a "trust or company service provider licence" under the AMLO
- (iv) is a wholly owned subsidiary of the Platform Operator

**"Client"** means a person to whom the Platform Operator provides services in the course of carrying out the Relevant Activities.

**"Client asset"** means client virtual assets and client money.

**"Client money"** means any money:
- (a) received or held by or on behalf of the Platform Operator; or
- (b) received or held by or on behalf of the Associated Entity
which is so received or held on behalf of a client or in which a client has a legal or equitable interest, and includes any accretions thereto whether as capital or income.

**"Client virtual asset"** means any virtual asset:
- (a) received or held by or on behalf of the Platform Operator; or
- (b) received or held by or on behalf of the Associated Entity
which is so received or held on behalf of a client or in which a client has a legal or equitable interest, and includes any rights thereto.

**"Platform Operator"** means:
- (a) a corporation which is granted a licence for Type 1 (dealing in securities) and Type 7 (providing automated trading services) regulated activities under section 116 of the SFO and carries out any Relevant Activities (SFO-licensed Platform Operator); and/or
- (b) a corporation which is granted a licence for providing a VA service under section 53ZRK of the AMLO and carries out any Relevant Activities.

**"Professional investor"** has the meaning as defined in section 1 of Part 1 of Schedule 1 to the SFO.

**"Retail client"** or **"retail investor"** means any person other than a professional investor.

**"Virtual asset"** or **"VA"** or **"token"** means:
- (a) any "virtual asset" as defined in section 53ZRA of the AMLO; and
- (b) any security token.

### Application

1.2 These Guidelines are applicable to all Platform Operators (whether they are licensed under the SFO and/or the AMLO) when they carry on Relevant Activities.

1.3 Parts II and III of these Guidelines are also applicable to:
- (a) a corporation which applies for a licence to become a Platform Operator
- (b) an individual who applies for a licence to become an LR
- (c) an individual who applies for approval to become an RO

## II. Fitness and Properness Requirements

### 2.1 Assessment Criteria

Persons applying to become a licensed person must satisfy the SFC that they are fit and proper to be licensed. When assessing fitness and properness, the SFC shall have regard to:

- (a) financial status or solvency
- (b) educational or other qualifications or experience
- (c) ability to carry on the Relevant Activities competently, honestly and fairly
- (d) reputation, character, reliability and financial integrity

### 2.5 Financial Status or Solvency

The SFC is not likely to be satisfied that a person is fit and proper if that person:

**For individuals:**
- is an undischarged bankrupt
- is subject to receivership or similar proceedings
- has failed to meet any judgment debt

**For corporations:**
- is subject to receivership, administration, liquidation or similar proceedings
- has failed to meet any judgment debt
- is unable to meet any financial or capital requirements

### 2.8 Reputation, Character, Reliability and Financial Integrity

Instances that might result in failure to meet this test:
- Found by a court to be involved in fraud, dishonesty or misfeasance
- Convicted of relevant criminal offences
- Censured, disciplined or disqualified by any professional or regulatory body
- Disqualified from being a director
- Found culpable of market misconduct by the Market Misconduct Tribunal

## III. Competence Requirements

### 3.4 Requirements for Corporations

Key elements considered:
- Business model and target clientele
- Corporate governance
- Internal controls
- Operational review
- Risk management
- Compliance arrangements

### Staff Competencies

- All supervisory staff should have **not less than 3 years** of relevant experience
- Appropriate qualifications required
- Arrangements to ensure compliance with CPT requirements

---

## IV. Continuous Professional Training Requirements

### 4.1 CPT Hours

**Licensed Representatives:**
- Minimum **10 CPT hours** per calendar year
- At least **5 hours** on topics related to VA, regulatory compliance and ethics

**Responsible Officers:**
- Minimum **12 CPT hours** per calendar year
- At least **2 hours** on regulatory compliance and ethical standards

### 4.4 CPT Topics

Relevant topics include:
- (a) virtual asset regulatory and legal developments
- (b) risk management for virtual assets
- (c) anti-money laundering and counter-financing of terrorism
- (d) ethics and conduct
- (e) technical and operational developments in virtual assets

---

## V. Conduct of Business Principles

### General Principles

Platform Operators should:
- Act honestly, fairly and in the best interests of clients
- Act with due skill, care and diligence
- Have adequate resources and procedures for proper performance
- Seek from clients information relevant to services provided
- Make adequate disclosure of relevant information to clients
- Endeavour to avoid conflicts of interest
- Comply with all applicable regulatory requirements
- Deal with the SFC in an open and cooperative manner

---

## VI. Financial Soundness

### 6.1 Liquid Capital

Platform Operators must always maintain liquid capital of not less than the higher of:
- (a) its Required Liquid Capital; and
- (b) HK$3,000,000 (approximately US$384,000)

### 6.3 Reporting Obligations

Platform Operators must submit:
- Monthly financial returns within 3 weeks after month-end
- Immediately notify the SFC if liquid capital falls below required levels

---

## VII. Operations

### 7.1 Operational Requirements

Platform Operators should:
- Establish clear internal policies and procedures
- Maintain a proper audit trail
- Implement effective risk management systems
- Ensure business continuity arrangements

### 7.4 Trading Rules

Platform Operators must:
- Have trading rules that are fair and transparent
- Clearly disclose trading rules to clients
- Apply trading rules consistently

### 7.6 Token Admission

Before admitting a virtual asset for trading, Platform Operators must conduct due diligence including:
- Background of the management team
- Regulatory status
- Supply and demand
- Technical aspects
- Legal and compliance aspects
- Market and liquidity factors

---

## VIII. Prevention of Market Manipulative and Abusive Activities

### 8.1 Requirements

Platform Operators should:
- Establish and implement written policies to identify, prevent, detect and report market misconduct
- Conduct surveillance on trading activities
- Report suspicious activities

---

## IX. Dealing with Clients

### 9.1 Know Your Client

Platform Operators must:
- Obtain sufficient information about clients
- Assess clients' knowledge and experience
- Ensure suitability of services

### 9.5 Client Agreement

Must include:
- Description of services
- Fee structure
- Risk disclosures
- Terms and conditions

### 9.8 Retail Clients - Additional Requirements

For retail clients, Platform Operators must:
- Conduct knowledge assessment
- Impose trading limits
- Only offer VAs that have been approved by the SFC for retail trading

### 9.9 VA for Retail Trading Requirements

To be eligible for retail trading, a virtual asset must be:
- A "large-cap virtual asset" (top 50 by market capitalization on two acceptable indices)
- Subject to SFC approval

---

## X. Custody of Client Assets

### 10.1 Segregation

Platform Operators must:
- Segregate client assets from proprietary assets
- Hold client assets on trust for clients

### 10.4 Cold Wallet Storage

Platform Operators must:
- Store no less than **98%** of client virtual assets in cold wallets
- Implement multi-signature or similar controls

### 10.6 Insurance/Compensation Arrangements

Platform Operators should maintain compensation arrangements (e.g., insurance) to cover:
- **100%** of client virtual assets held in cold storage
- **50%** of client virtual assets held in hot/warm storage

---

## XI. Management, Supervision and Internal Control

### 11.1 Management Structure

Platform Operators must have:
- Clear organizational structure
- Defined responsibilities
- Adequate staffing levels

### 11.3 Managers-In-Charge (MICs)

Platform Operators must appoint MICs for each of the following core functions:
1. Overall Management Oversight
2. Key Business Line
3. Operational Control and Review
4. Risk Management
5. Finance and Accounting
6. Information Technology
7. Compliance
8. Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Financing of Terrorism

---

## XII. Cybersecurity

### 12.1 Cybersecurity Governance

Platform Operators should:
- Appoint a qualified chief information security officer
- Establish a cybersecurity governance framework
- Conduct regular security assessments

### 12.4 Security Controls

Key requirements:
- Multi-factor authentication for critical systems
- Encryption of sensitive data
- Regular penetration testing
- Intrusion detection systems

### 12.7 Incident Management

Platform Operators must:
- Have incident response plans
- Report security incidents to the SFC
- Conduct post-incident reviews

---

## XIII. Conflicts of Interest

### 13.1 Identification and Management

Platform Operators should:
- Identify potential conflicts of interest
- Implement measures to manage conflicts
- Disclose conflicts to clients where appropriate

### 13.3 Prohibited Activities

Platform Operators should not:
- Engage in proprietary trading on their own platform
- Front-run client orders

---

## XIV. Record Keeping

### 14.1 Record Retention

Platform Operators must retain records for at least **7 years** including:
- Client account records
- Transaction records
- Order books
- Audit trails
- Communications with clients

### 14.3 Format

Records should be:
- Readily accessible
- Capable of being reproduced in hard copy
- Protected from unauthorized access

---

## XV. Auditors

### 15.1 Appointment

Platform Operators must:
- Appoint an external auditor
- Ensure auditor has appropriate expertise in virtual assets

### 15.2 Audit Requirements

Annual audit covering:
- Financial statements
- Compliance with regulatory requirements
- Internal controls

---

## XVI. Ongoing Reporting and Notification Obligations

### 16.1 Notification Requirements

Platform Operators must notify the SFC of:
- Material changes to business
- Security incidents
- Regulatory issues
- Changes to key personnel

### 16.2 Regular Reporting

Submit to SFC:
- Monthly financial returns
- Annual audited accounts
- Quarterly reports on trading activities

## Key Thresholds Summary

| Requirement | Threshold |
|------------|-----------|
| Minimum liquid capital | HK$3,000,000 |
| Cold wallet storage | >= 98% of client VA |
| Insurance coverage (cold storage) | 100% |
| Insurance coverage (hot storage) | 50% |
| Record retention period | 7 years |
| LR minimum CPT hours | 10 hours/year |
| RO minimum CPT hours | 12 hours/year |
| Supervisory staff experience | 3+ years |
