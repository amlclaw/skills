## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Legal Basis

This Guideline is issued by the Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) under:
- Section 171 of the Stablecoins Ordinance, Cap. [-]
- Section 7 of the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance, Cap. 615 (AMLO)

### 1.2 Purpose

This Guideline provides guidance to assist a licensee to understand:
- The money laundering and terrorist financing (ML/TF) risks associated with regulated stablecoin activities
- The relevant anti-money laundering and counter-financing of terrorism (AML/CFT) regulatory requirements

### 1.3 Applicability

A licensee is a stablecoin issuer which holds a licence granted under section 15 of the Stablecoins Ordinance. A licensee is a financial institution (FI) as defined in Part 2 of Schedule 1 to the AMLO.

### 1.4 Enforcement

Compliance with this Guideline is enforced through the Stablecoins Ordinance and the AMLO. A licensee who fails to comply may be subject to disciplinary or other actions.

### 1.5 Mandatory Requirements

The use of the word "must" or "should" in this Guideline indicates a mandatory requirement.

## 2. Risk Assessment

### 2.1 Risk-Based Approach

A licensee should adopt a risk-based approach (RBA) in the design and implementation of its AML/CFT policies, procedures and controls ("AML/CFT Systems").

### 2.2 Institutional ML/TF Risk Assessment

A licensee should conduct an institutional ML/TF risk assessment considering:
- Customer risk factors
- Country risk factors
- Product, service or transaction risk factors
- Delivery channel risk factors

### 2.3 Risk Assessment Requirements

In undertaking an institutional ML/TF risk assessment, a licensee should ensure that:
- The process is appropriately structured and properly documented
- All relevant risk factors are taken into account
- Senior management approval is obtained
- A process is in place to keep the assessment up-to-date
- Mechanisms are in place to provide risk assessment to the HKMA when required

### 2.4 Scale and Scope

The scale and scope should be commensurate with the nature, size and complexity of the licensee's business. It should also consider:
- Hong Kong's jurisdiction-wide ML/TF risk assessment
- Higher risks notified by the HKMA, JFIU or other law enforcement agencies

### 2.5 New Products and Technologies

A licensee should identify and assess ML/TF risks in relation to:
- Development of new products and new business practices
- Use of new or developing technologies

## 3. AML/CFT Systems

### 3.1 General Requirements

A licensee should:
- Have AML/CFT Systems approved by senior management
- Monitor implementation and enhance if necessary
- Take enhanced measures where higher risks are identified

### 3.2 Simplified Measures

AML/CFT Systems may be simplified provided that:
- The licensee complies with statutory requirements
- Lower ML/TF risks have been identified through risk assessment
- Simplified systems are approved by senior management
- Never simplified when there is a suspicion of ML/TF

### 3.3 Components of AML/CFT Systems

- Compliance management arrangements
- Independent audit function
- Employee screening procedures
- Ongoing employee training programme

### 3.4-3.5 Compliance Management

Effective ML/TF risk management requires adequate governance arrangements including:
- Oversight by board of directors and senior management
- Appointment of a Compliance Officer (CO) at management level
- Appointment of a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)

### 3.6 Independent Audit Function

A licensee should establish an independent audit function with:
- Direct line of communication to senior management and board
- Sufficient expertise and resources
- Ability to undertake independent reviews of AML/CFT Systems

### 3.7 Employee Screening

Adequate and appropriate screening procedures to ensure only employees with high integrity are deployed.

### 3.8 Ongoing Employee Training

Training should be tailored according to:
- Job functions
- Responsibilities
- Level of experience

### 3.9-3.10 Group-wide AML/CFT Systems

A licensee with branches or subsidiary undertakings outside Hong Kong should implement group-wide AML/CFT Systems.

## 4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 4.1 When CDD is Required

CDD measures must be performed when:
- Establishing business relations
- Carrying out occasional transactions
- There is suspicion of ML/TF
- There are doubts about previously obtained information

### 4.2 CDD Measures

A licensee must:
- Identify the customer and verify identity
- Identify the beneficial owner
- Obtain information on the purpose and intended nature of business relations
- Conduct ongoing monitoring

### 4.3 Verification Methods

Identity verification may use:
- Documentary methods
- Non-documentary methods (electronic verification)

### 4.4 Beneficial Owner Identification

For legal persons: identify natural persons who ultimately own or control the entity
For trusts: identify settlors, trustees, protectors, beneficiaries

### 4.5 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Required for:
- Higher risk customers
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Correspondent relationships
- Complex or unusual transactions

## 5. Ongoing Monitoring

### 5.1 Transaction Monitoring

A licensee should monitor transactions to detect:
- Unusual or suspicious activities
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile
- Transactions with high-risk jurisdictions

### 5.2 Customer Information Updates

A licensee should ensure customer information is kept up-to-date and relevant.

---

## 6. Stablecoin Transfers

### 6.1 Travel Rule Requirements

A licensee must obtain and transmit required originator and beneficiary information for stablecoin transfers.

### 6.2 Required Information

**For Originator:**
- Name
- Account number/wallet address
- Address, national identity number, or date/place of birth

**For Beneficiary:**
- Name
- Account number/wallet address

### 6.3 Threshold

Travel rule applies to transfers above the applicable threshold.

### 6.4 Counterparty Due Diligence

A licensee should conduct due diligence on counterparty institutions.

---

## 7. Terrorist Financing, Financial Sanctions and Proliferation Financing

### 7.1 Sanctions Screening

A licensee must screen customers against:
- UN sanctions lists
- Hong Kong designated entities lists
- Other applicable sanctions lists

### 7.2 Freezing Actions

A licensee must take immediate action to freeze assets of designated persons.

---

## 8. Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs)

### 8.1 Obligation to Report

A licensee must file STRs with the Joint Financial Intelligence Unit (JFIU) when:
- There are grounds for suspicion of ML/TF
- Transactions involve proceeds of crime

### 8.2 Timing

STRs should be filed as soon as reasonably practicable.

---

## 9. Record-keeping

### 9.1 Record Retention

A licensee must keep records for at least 5 years after:
- End of business relationship
- Date of occasional transaction

### 9.2 Types of Records

- Customer identification records
- Transaction records
- Suspicious transaction reports
- Risk assessments

## Key Definitions

- **Licensee**: Stablecoin issuer licensed under section 15 of the Stablecoins Ordinance
- **ML/TF**: Money laundering and terrorist financing
- **RBA**: Risk-based approach
- **CDD**: Customer due diligence
- **EDD**: Enhanced due diligence
- **STR**: Suspicious transaction report
- **JFIU**: Joint Financial Intelligence Unit
