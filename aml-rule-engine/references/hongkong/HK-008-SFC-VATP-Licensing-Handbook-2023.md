## Foreword

1. This Handbook provides general information of licensing matters in relation to virtual asset trading platform operators under the Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO) (Chapter 571) and/or the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance (AMLO) (Chapter 615) administered by the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC).

2. The terms of this Handbook follow those used in the "Guidelines for Virtual Asset Trading Platform Operators" unless otherwise defined.

3. Any person carrying on a regulated activity and/or VA service in Hong Kong has to be licensed by the SFC.

4. **It is a serious offence to carry on a regulated activity and/or VA service in Hong Kong or actively market to the investing public of Hong Kong any services which constitute a regulated activity and/or VA service without the required licence(s).**

5. The SFC maintains a register of Platform Operators and their licensed representatives at http://www.sfc.hk/publicregWeb/searchByName

## Part I: Do you need a licence?

### Chapter 1: Licensing Requirements

#### 1.1 General Requirements

You need to be licensed for:
- Type 1 (dealing in securities) and Type 7 (providing automated trading services) regulated activities under section 116 of the SFO; and/or
- Providing a VA service under section 53ZRK of the AMLO

If:
- You are a corporation carrying on a business in the activities set out above in Hong Kong
- You are a corporation actively marketing to the public any services that would constitute the activities set out above
- You are an individual performing a regulated function for your principal which is a Platform Operator

**Note:** Sole proprietorship or partnership is not an acceptable form of business structure for licensing purposes.

#### 1.2 Further Guidance

**Conducting business outside Hong Kong:**
A licence issued by the SFC only allows the holder to carry on business in the Relevant Activities in Hong Kong. When conducting activities outside Hong Kong, ensure full compliance with the relevant legal and regulatory requirements of that jurisdiction.

**SFC Regulatory Sandbox:**
Platform Operators demonstrating genuine commitment to carry on Relevant Activities through innovative Fintech may operate in a confined regulatory environment.

## Part II: What are the requirements for getting a licence?

### Chapter 3: Platform Operators

#### 3.1 General Fit and Proper Requirement

To be considered fit and proper, an applicant must demonstrate:
- Financial soundness
- Competence and capability
- Integrity and honesty
- Appropriate organizational structure

#### 3.2 Specific Approval Criteria

**Organizational Structure:**
- Clear lines of responsibility
- Adequate staffing
- Proper segregation of duties

**Financial Resources:**
- Minimum paid-up share capital of HK$5,000,000
- Maintain liquid capital in compliance with the Financial Resources Rules

**Responsible Officers:**
- At least 2 responsible officers
- At least one RO must be an executive director
- At least one RO must be ordinarily resident in Hong Kong

**Experience Requirements:**
- Responsible officers should have at least 3 years of relevant experience
- Management personnel should have appropriate qualifications

#### 3.3 Use of External Electronic Data Storage

Platform Operators may use external electronic data storage providers subject to:
- Prior approval from the SFC
- The provider being in an acceptable jurisdiction
- Adequate security measures

---

### Chapter 4: Responsible Officers

#### 4.1 General Fit and Proper Requirement

Responsible officers must meet the same fit and proper criteria as Platform Operators.

#### 4.2 Specific Approval Criteria

- At least 3 years of relevant experience in the past 6 years
- Competent to perform the functions of a responsible officer
- Sufficient authority within the Platform Operator

#### 4.3 Sufficient Authority

A responsible officer must have sufficient authority to:
- Supervise the Platform Operator's business
- Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements
- Make decisions on behalf of the Platform Operator

---

### Chapter 5: Licensed Representatives

#### 5.1 General Fit and Proper Requirement

Licensed representatives must demonstrate:
- Competence in the regulated activity/VA service
- Good character and integrity
- Appropriate qualifications

#### 5.2 Specific Approval Criteria

**Academic Qualifications:**
- Degree from a recognized institution, or
- Equivalent professional qualifications

**Industry Qualifications:**
- Pass the relevant licensing examinations, or
- Have relevant experience waiving the examination requirement

**Relevant Experience:**
- At least 2 years in the past 5 years for experienced practitioners

## Part III: How to apply for a licence?

### Chapter 6: Application Procedures

#### 6.1 WINGS

All licence applications must be submitted through the SFC's Web-based INtegrated Service (WINGS) at https://wings.sfc.hk

#### 6.2 Completing the Application Form

Forms required:
- Form 1 - Application for licence (corporations)
- Form 3 - Application for licence (individuals)
- Various supplements depending on circumstances

#### 6.3 Submitting the Application

Applications should include:
- Completed application forms
- Supporting documents
- Application fees

#### 6.7 Processing Time

The SFC aims to process applications within:
- 3-4 months for straightforward cases
- Longer for complex applications

#### 6.8 Refusal of Application

The SFC may refuse an application if:
- The applicant is not fit and proper
- The applicant fails to meet licensing requirements
- Granting the licence is not in the public interest

## Part IV: After being licensed

### Chapter 8: Ongoing Obligations

#### 8.2 Availability of Responsible Officers

Platform Operators must ensure that:
- At least one responsible officer is available at all times during business hours
- There are at least 2 responsible officers at all times

#### 8.3 Cessation of Business

Platform Operators must:
- Notify the SFC at least 1 month before ceasing business
- Make arrangements for client asset protection

#### 8.7 Summary of Notification Requirements

Notify the SFC of:
- Changes to key personnel
- Changes to substantial shareholders
- Material changes to business
- Legal proceedings
- Disciplinary actions
- Breaches of regulatory requirements

**Notification timeframes:**
- Immediately: serious matters
- 7 business days: other material matters

#### 8.8 Submission of Audited Accounts

Platform Operators must submit:
- Audited financial statements within 4 months of financial year-end

#### 8.9 Submission of Financial Resources Returns

Submit monthly returns within 3 weeks after month-end.

#### 8.10 Payment of Annual Fees

Annual licence fees are payable within 1 month of the anniversary of the licence grant date.

**Fee Schedule:**
- Platform Operators: HK$154,280 per year
- Licensed Representatives: HK$2,850 per year

#### 8.12 Continuous Professional Training (CPT)

Licensed individuals must complete CPT requirements as specified in the Guidelines for Virtual Asset Trading Platform Operators.
