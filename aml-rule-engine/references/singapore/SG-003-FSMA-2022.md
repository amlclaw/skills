# Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 (FSMA)

The Financial Services and Markets Act (FSMA) is an omnibus Act for the sector-wide regulation of financial services and markets. Parliament passed the FSMA on 5 April 2022.

## Key Topics

- Anti-Money Laundering
- AML/CFT
- Fintech
- Digital Currencies
- Fit and Proper Criteria
- Licensing, Authorisation and Registration
- Prudential and Supervisory Requirements
- Resolution
- Risk Management
- Technology Risk

## Applies to

- Banks and merchant banks
- Finance companies
- Payment Services Act licensees
- Insurers and insurance intermediaries
- Financial advisers
- Capital markets services licensees
- Licensed trust companies
- Credit card or charge card issuers
- Operators of designated payment systems
- **Providers of digital token service licensed under the FSMA**

---

## Implementation Phases

### Phase 1 (Commenced 28 April 2023)
- General powers over financial institutions (Parts 2, 10, 11 and 12)
- Anti-Money Laundering / Countering the Financing of Terrorism (Part 4)
- Financial Dispute Resolution Schemes (Part 6)

### Phase 2A (Commenced 10 May 2024)
- Technology and risk management (Part 5)
- Control and resolution of financial institutions (Parts 7 and 8)

### Phase 2B (Commenced 31 July 2024)
- Harmonised and expanded power to issue prohibition orders (Part 3)

### Phase 3 (Commenced 30 June 2025)
- **Framework to regulate digital token service providers (Part 9)**
- Applies to DTSPs established/incorporated in Singapore providing digital token services outside Singapore

---

## Part 9 - Digital Token Service Providers (DTSP)

Phase 3 implements the framework to regulate digital token service providers:
- Targets DTSPs established or incorporated in Singapore
- Covers digital token services provided outside Singapore
- Aligns with FATF guidelines for Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs)

### Licensing Requirement
Pursuant to section 137 of the FSM Act, any person conducting digital token services in Singapore must hold a licence unless exempted.

## Key Features

1. **Omnibus Legislation**: Consolidates sector-wide regulation of financial services
2. **Technology Risk Management**: New provisions on technology and risk management
3. **Digital Assets**: Framework for regulating digital token service providers
4. **Resolution Powers**: Enhanced powers for control and resolution of financial institutions
5. **AML/CFT**: Comprehensive anti-money laundering provisions migrated from MAS Act
