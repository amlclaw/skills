# MAS Notice PSN02

## Prevention of Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism - Holders of Payment Services Licence (Digital Payment Token Service)

---

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Legal Basis

This Notice is issued under section 16 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 ("FSM Act") and applies to all holders of a payment services licence under the Payment Services Act 2019 ("PS Act") that carry on a business of providing a digital payment token service.

### 1.2 Effective Date

This Notice shall take effect from 4 April 2024, with amendments taking effect 1 July 2025.

---

## 2. Definitions

### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **AML/CFT** | Anti-money laundering and countering the financing of terrorism |
| **Authority** | Monetary Authority of Singapore |
| **beneficial owner** | Natural person who ultimately owns or controls the customer or on whose behalf a transaction is conducted |
| **beneficiary institution** | Financial institution that receives value transfer from ordering institution |
| **business relations** | Opening or maintenance of an account for accepting, processing or executing transactions |
| **CDD measures** | Customer due diligence measures required by paragraph 6 |
| **CDSA** | Corruption, Drug Trafficking and Other Serious Crimes (Confiscation of Benefits) Act 1992 |
| **customer** | Person with business relations established or for whom transactions are undertaken |
| **FATF** | Financial Action Task Force |
| **financial group** | Group consisting of legal person exercising control functions and its branches and subsidiaries |
| **ordering institution** | Financial institution that initiates value transfer |
| **STR** | Suspicious transaction report |
| **STRO** | Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office |
| **TSOFA** | Terrorism (Suppression of Financing) Act 2002 |
| **value transfer** | Transaction carried out on behalf of originator to make digital payment tokens available to beneficiary |

### 2.2 Currency Equivalents

Any threshold expressed in S$ includes equivalent amount in any other currency and in any digital payment token.

## 3. Underlying Principles

### 3.1 Core Principles

- Exercise due diligence when dealing with customers
- Conduct business in conformity with high ethical standards
- Guard against business relations that may facilitate money laundering or terrorism financing
- Assist and cooperate with law enforcement authorities

---

## 4. Assessing Risks and Applying a Risk-Based Approach

### 4.1 Risk Assessment

A payment service provider shall take appropriate steps to identify, assess and understand its money laundering and terrorism financing risks in relation to:
- Its customers
- Countries or jurisdictions customers are from or in
- Countries or jurisdictions of operations
- Products, services, transactions and delivery channels

### 4.2 Documentation Requirements

- Document risk assessments
- Consider all relevant risk factors
- Keep risk assessments up-to-date
- Have mechanisms to provide information to Authority

### 4.3 Risk Mitigation

Payment service providers shall:
- Develop and implement policies, procedures and controls approved by senior management
- Monitor implementation and enhance if necessary
- Perform enhanced measures for higher risks
- Address risk assessment and guidance from Authority

---

## 5. New Products, Practices and Technologies

### 5.1-5.2 Risk Assessment Requirements

Payment service providers shall:
- Identify and assess ML/TF risks for new products, practices and technologies
- Undertake risk assessments prior to launch
- Take appropriate measures to manage and mitigate risks

### 5.3 Anonymity Concerns

Special attention shall be paid to products, practices and technologies that favour anonymity.

---

## 6. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 6.1 Anonymous Accounts Prohibited

No payment service provider shall open or maintain an anonymous account or account in fictitious name.

### 6.2 Suspicious Activity Prior to Business Relations

Where there are reasonable grounds to suspect assets are proceeds of crime:
- Do not establish business relations
- File STR and extend copy to Authority upon request

### 6.3 When CDD Required

CDD measures required when:
- Establishing business relations
- Undertaking transactions without account
- Suspicion of ML/TF
- Doubts about previously obtained information
- Transaction above applicable threshold

### CDD Measures Include:

1. **Customer Identification**
   - Obtain full name
   - Obtain unique identification number
   - Verify identity using reliable documents or electronic verification

2. **Beneficial Owner Identification**
   - Identify natural persons who ultimately own or control customer
   - For legal persons: persons with >25% ownership or control
   - For legal arrangements: identify relevant parties

3. **Purpose of Business Relations**
   - Obtain information on intended purpose and nature

4. **Ongoing Monitoring**
   - Monitor transactions
   - Keep records up-to-date

---

## 7. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

### 7.1 When Required

Enhanced measures required for:
- Higher risk customers
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Correspondent relationships
- Complex or unusual transactions
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions

### 7.2 EDD Measures

- Obtain additional information on customer
- Obtain information on source of wealth and funds
- Conduct enhanced ongoing monitoring
- Obtain senior management approval

---

## 8. Value Transfers (Travel Rule)

### 8.1 Requirements

Prior to executing value transfer, obtain and transmit:

**Originator Information:**
- Name
- Account number or unique transaction reference
- Address, national identity number, or date/place of birth

**Beneficiary Information:**
- Name
- Account number or unique transaction reference

### 8.2 Threshold

Applies to transfers of S$1,500 or more.

### 8.3 Ordering Institution Obligations

- Obtain and verify originator information
- Include required information with transfer
- Make information available to beneficiary institution

### 8.4 Beneficiary Institution Obligations

- Identify transfers lacking required information
- Take risk-based measures for incomplete transfers
- Consider filing STR for repeated failures

---

## 9. Suspicious Transaction Reporting

### 9.1 Obligation

Payment service providers must file STR with STRO when there is reasonable grounds to suspect:
- Property represents proceeds of crime
- Transaction is related to ML/TF

### 9.2 Timing

Report as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 9.3 No Tipping Off

Do not disclose to customer that STR has been filed.

---

## 10. Record Keeping

### 10.1 Retention Period

Records must be kept for at least 5 years after:
- End of business relationship
- Date of transaction

### 10.2 Records Required

- Customer identification records
- Transaction records
- Suspicious transaction reports
- Correspondence with customers
- Risk assessments

---

## 11. Targeted Financial Sanctions

### 11.1 Screening

Payment service providers must screen against:
- UN sanctions lists
- Singapore designated entities lists
- Other applicable sanctions

### 11.2 Freezing

Take immediate action to freeze assets of designated persons.

### 11.3 Prohibition

Do not deal with property of designated persons.

---

## 12. Internal Policies, Procedures and Controls

### 12.1 Requirements

Payment service providers shall establish:
- Written internal policies, procedures and controls
- Compliance management arrangements
- Audit function
- Screening procedures
- Training programs

### 12.2 Senior Management Responsibility

Senior management shall be responsible for AML/CFT compliance.

### 12.3 Compliance Officer

Appoint qualified compliance officer to oversee AML/CFT program.

---

## Important Notes

- Money laundering includes proliferation financing
- All references to money laundering risks include proliferation financing risks
- Payment service providers must cooperate with Authority examinations
