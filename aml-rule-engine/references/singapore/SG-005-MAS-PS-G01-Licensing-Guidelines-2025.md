## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Scope

These Guidelines provide guidance on:
- Application procedures
- Licensing criteria
- Ongoing requirements

For payment service providers under the Payment Services Act 2019 ("PS Act").

### 1.2 Related Legislation

These Guidelines should be read in conjunction with:
- Payment Services Act
- Payment Services Regulations (PSR)
- Other relevant notices, guidelines and FAQs issued by MAS

## 2. Licences under the PS Act

### 2.1 Licensing Requirement

Pursuant to section 5 of the PS Act, any person that conducts payment services in Singapore as defined in the First Schedule is required to hold a licence unless exempted.

Section 13 sets out applicable exemptions from holding a licence.

### 2.2 Types of Payment Services

| Activity Type | Service | Description |
|--------------|---------|-------------|
| Activity A | Account issuance service | Issuing payment accounts (e-wallets, non-bank credit cards) |
| Activity B | Domestic money transfer service | Local funds transfer in Singapore (payment gateways, kiosks) |
| Activity C | Cross-border money transfer service | Inbound/outbound remittance services |
| Activity D | Merchant acquisition service | Processing payment transactions for merchants |
| Activity E | E-money issuance service | Issuing e-money for payments or transfers |
| Activity F | Digital payment token service | Buying/selling DPTs, exchange platforms, transmission, custody |
| Activity G | Money-changing service | Buying/selling foreign currency notes |

### 2.3 Types of Licences

Three types of licences under section 6 of PS Act:

| Licence Type | Applicable To |
|-------------|---------------|
| Money-Changing (MC) | Only money-changing service |
| Standard Payment Institution (SPI) | Payment services below specified thresholds |
| Major Payment Institution (MPI) | Payment services without threshold restrictions |

### 2.4 Specified Thresholds (Section 6(5))

- S$3m monthly transactions for any single activity type
- S$6m monthly transactions for two or more activity types
- S$5m daily outstanding e-money

### 2.5 Licence Variation

Licensees must apply for variation in Form 2 if intending to:
- Add or remove any payment service
- Change licence type

## 3. Admission Criteria

### 3.1 Governance and Ownership

- Must comply with governance and ownership structure in Appendix 1
- Must be registered with ACRA

### 3.2 Fit and Proper Requirements

Applicant must satisfy MAS that the following are fit and proper:
- Sole-proprietor, partners, or directors and CEO
- Shareholders and employees
- The applicant itself

Per Guidelines on Fit and Proper Criteria [FSG-G01].

**Considerations:**
- Honesty, integrity and reputation
- Competence and capability
- Financial soundness
- Conflict of interest
- Time commitment
- No adverse reputation regarding financial crime

### 3.3 Competency of Key Individuals

Key individuals must have:
- Sufficient experience in payment services or related financial services
- Understanding of Singapore's regulatory framework
- Relevant experience for managing sizeable teams (where applicable)

### 3.4 Permanent Place of Business

Must have permanent place of business or registered office in Singapore:
- Physical office for book and record keeping
- At least one person for customer queries
- Ability to address authority inquiries

### 3.5 Base Capital Requirements

| Licence Type | Base Capital |
|-------------|-------------|
| Money-Changing (MC) | S$100,000 |
| Standard Payment Institution (SPI) | S$100,000 |
| Major Payment Institution (MPI) | S$250,000 |

**For DPT Service Providers:**
- SPI: S$100,000
- MPI: S$250,000

### 3.6 Compliance Arrangements

Must have effective compliance arrangements with adequate resources commensurate with nature, scale and complexity of business.

### 3.7 Technology Risk Management

Must have adequate IT systems and controls for:
- Customer protection
- Transaction security
- Data integrity

### 3.8 Audit Arrangements

Adequate independent audit arrangements for assessing adequacy and effectiveness of procedures and controls.

## 4. Licence Application Requirements

### 4.1 Application Process

- Submit via MASNet portal
- Use prescribed forms (Form 1 for new applications)
- Include all supporting documents

### 4.2 Required Documents

- Business plan
- Organization structure
- Financial projections
- Compliance policies
- Technology risk assessment
- AML/CFT policies

### 4.3 Processing Time

MAS aims to process complete applications within:
- 3-6 months for straightforward cases
- Longer for complex applications

---

## 5. Ongoing Requirements for Licensees

### 5.1 Notification Requirements

Licensees must notify MAS of:
- Changes in key personnel
- Material changes to business
- Breaches of requirements
- Financial difficulties

### 5.2 Annual Requirements

- Submit annual returns
- Maintain adequate capital
- Submit audited financial statements

### 5.3 AML/CFT Compliance

Must comply with:
- MAS Notice PSN01 (for non-DPT services)
- MAS Notice PSN02 (for DPT services)
- All applicable AML/CFT requirements
