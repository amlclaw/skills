# Guidelines on Licensing for Digital Token Service Providers

## Financial Services and Markets Act 2022

---

## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Scope

These Guidelines provide guidance on:
- Application procedures
- Licensing criteria
- Ongoing requirements

For Digital Token Service Providers ("DTSPs") under Part 9 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 ("FSM Act").

### 1.2 Definition of DTSP

DTSPs are individuals, partnerships or Singapore corporations that are:
- Operating from a place of business in Singapore, OR
- Formed or incorporated in Singapore but carry on a business of providing digital token services outside Singapore

### 1.3 Related Legislation

These Guidelines should be read with:
- FSM Act
- Financial Services and Markets (Digital Token Service Provider) Regulations ("FSM Regulations")
- Other relevant notices, guidelines and FAQs issued by MAS

---

## 2. Licence under the FSM Act

### 2.1 Licensing Requirement

Pursuant to section 137 of the FSM Act, any person conducting digital token services in Singapore must hold a licence unless exempted.

### 2.2 Transitional Arrangement

MAS will not be providing a transitional arrangement for DTSPs. DTSPs must suspend or cease carrying on business by 30 June 2025.

### 2.3 Types of Digital Token Services

Applicants should consider whether activities fall within the ten digital token services in the First Schedule of the FSM Act.

---

## 3. Admission Criteria

### 3.1 MAS Approach

DTSPs are susceptible to higher money laundering, terrorism financing and proliferation financing ("ML/TF") risks. MAS approaches licensing in a prudent and cautious manner.

### Extremely Limited Circumstances for Licence Grant:

- Applicant has a business model that makes economic sense
- Applicant can demonstrate valid reasons for not providing services in Singapore
- Applicant is already regulated and supervised for compliance with international standards (FSB, IOSCO, FATF)
- MAS has no concerns with business structure

### 3.2 Criteria Requirements

#### 3.2.1 Governance and Ownership Requirements
Must comply with governance and ownership structure and be registered with ACRA.

#### 3.2.2 Fit and Proper
Applicant must satisfy MAS that:
- Sole-proprietor, partners, managers, directors and CEO
- Shareholders and employees
- The applicant itself

Are fit and proper per Guidelines on Fit and Proper Criteria [FSG-G01].

**Considerations:**
- Honesty, integrity and reputation
- Competence and capability
- Financial soundness
- Conflict of interest
- Time commitment
- Adverse reputation regarding financial crime and sanctions compliance

#### 3.2.3 Competency of Key Individuals
Key individuals must have:
- Sufficient experience in digital token services industry
- Understanding of Singapore regulatory framework
- Relevant experience for oversight of sizeable teams
- Educational qualifications and professional certification

#### 3.2.4 Permanent Place of Business
Must have permanent place of business or registered office in Singapore:
- Office area for secure book and record keeping
- At least one person present for customer queries
- Address inquiries from authorities

#### 3.2.5 Base Capital Requirements

| Business Type | Base Capital Requirement |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| Sole-proprietor | S$250,000 cash deposit with MAS |
| Partnership/LLP | S$250,000 total capital contribution |
| Corporation | S$250,000 base capital |

**Notes:**
- Capital buffer should cover 6-12 months operating expenses
- Effective monitoring process required

#### 3.2.6 Compliance Arrangements
Must have effective compliance arrangements with adequate resources commensurate with nature, scale and complexity of business.

#### 3.2.7 Technology Risk Management
Must perform penetration test over proposed services:
- Remediate all high-risk findings
- Conduct independent validation
- Complete prior to licence grant

#### 3.2.8 Audit Arrangements
Adequate independent audit arrangements for:
- Assessing adequacy and effectiveness of procedures and controls
- Compliance with regulatory requirements
- Commensurate with scale and complexity

#### 3.2.9 Annual Audit Requirements
Must meet annual audit requirements under section 158 of FSM Act.

#### 3.2.10 Letter of Responsibility/Undertaking
MAS may require letters from majority shareholders, parent company and/or related company.

### 3.3 Other Factors

MAS may consider:
- Track record and financial condition
- Operational readiness
- Risk awareness and mitigation
- Public interest

## 4. Licence Application Requirements

### 4.1 Application Guidance

Applicants should refer to Appendix 3 for required information.

### 4.1.1 Legal Opinion

New applicants must submit legal opinion from reputable law firm including:
- Clear summary of business model
- Assessment of whether services are regulated under FSM Act

### 4.1.3 External Auditor's Independent Assessment

Upon in-principle approval (IPA), applicant must:
- Appoint qualified independent External Auditor
- Perform independent assessment of Technology and Cybersecurity risks

---

## 5. Ongoing Requirements for Licensees

Licensees must maintain compliance with all applicable requirements on an ongoing basis.

## Key Points Summary

1. **High Bar for Licensing**: MAS takes a prudent and cautious approach; extremely limited circumstances for granting licences

2. **Base Capital**: Minimum S$250,000 required

3. **Fit and Proper**: Strict requirements for all key individuals

4. **Compliance**: Must have adequate compliance arrangements

5. **Technology Risk**: Penetration testing required before licence grant

6. **Audit**: Annual audit requirements must be met

7. **No Transitional Period**: DTSPs must comply by 30 June 2025
