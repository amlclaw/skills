# Singapore MAS Notice PSN02: AML/CFT Requirements for Digital Payment Token Service

**Purpose**: Binding AML/CFT requirements for DPT service providers in Singapore
**Key Assumptions**: Applies to all DPT licensees under Payment Services Act 2019
**Usage Patterns**: Travel Rule compliance; CDD requirements; transaction monitoring

---

## Overview

- **Notice**: MAS Notice PSN02
- **Authority**: Monetary Authority of Singapore
- **Legal Basis**: Section 16, Financial Services and Markets Act 2022
- **Applies to**: All payment services licensees providing DPT services
- **Latest Revision**: 30 June 2025
- **Source**: https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/notices/psn02-aml-cft-notice---digital-payment-token-service

---

## Part 1 - Scope and Definitions

### Application

Applies to all holders of payment services licence under Payment Services Act 2019 that carry on business of providing digital payment token service:
- Standard Payment Institution (SPI) licensees
- Major Payment Institution (MPI) licensees

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **DPT** | Digital payment token as defined under PS Act |
| **Value Transfer** | Any DPT transaction involving transfer of value between entities |
| **Ordering Institution** | Institution that initiates a value transfer |
| **Beneficiary Institution** | Institution that receives a value transfer |

---

## Part 2 - Customer Due Diligence

### CDD Triggers

CDD required when:
1. Establishing business relationship
2. Carrying out occasional transaction >= S$5,000
3. Suspecting money laundering or terrorism financing
4. Doubting accuracy of previously obtained information

### Standard CDD Measures

- Identify and verify customer identity
- Identify beneficial owners
- Understand purpose and intended nature of business relationship
- Ongoing monitoring of transactions

### Enhanced Due Diligence

Required for:
- Higher risk customers
- Politically exposed persons (PEPs)
- Customers from higher risk countries
- Complex or unusual transactions
- Non-face-to-face relationships

### Simplified Due Diligence

Permitted for lower risk situations as specified by MAS

## Part 3 - Travel Rule Requirements

### Application to DPT Transfers

All DPT transactions involving transfer of value treated as value transfers subject to regulatory obligations similar to traditional wire transfers.

### Threshold-Based Requirements

#### Transfers up to S$1,500

| Party | Required Information |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Originator** | Name, account/transaction reference |
| **Beneficiary** | Name, account/transaction reference |

#### Transfers exceeding S$1,500

| Party | Required Information |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Originator** | Name, account number, address OR national ID OR date and place of birth |
| **Beneficiary** | Name, account number |

### Ordering Institution Obligations

1. Identification and verification of value transfer originator
2. Accurate transmission of originator and beneficiary information
3. Secure and prompt delivery of information to beneficiary institutions
4. Full retention and disclosure capability to MAS and law enforcement

### Beneficiary Institution Obligations

1. Verify beneficiary information received
2. Implement risk-based procedures for handling incomplete information
3. Consider filing STR if information incomplete

---

## Part 4 - Record Keeping

### Retention Requirements

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Transaction records** | 5 years from transaction date |
| **CDD records** | 5 years from end of business relationship |
| **Account files** | 5 years from account closure |

### Record Content

Must include:
- Customer identification information
- Transaction details
- Results of any analysis undertaken
- Correspondence and communications

---

## Part 5 - Suspicious Transaction Reporting

### Reporting Obligation

File Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) with STRO when:
1. Know or have reasonable grounds to suspect ML/TF
2. Complex, unusual, or large transactions without apparent economic purpose

### Filing Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Authority** | Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office (STRO) |
| **Timeline** | As soon as reasonably practicable |
| **Format** | STRO prescribed form |

### Tipping-Off Prohibition

- Must not disclose STR filing to customer
- Must not alert customer to investigation
- Internal escalation permitted

---

## Part 6 - Sanctions Screening

### Required Screening

- Screen against UN sanctions lists
- Screen against MAS designated lists
- Screen against other applicable sanctions regimes

### Screening Timing

- Before establishing business relationship
- Before processing transactions
- Ongoing periodic rescreening

---

## Part 7 - Internal Controls

### Policies and Procedures

Must establish:
1. Written AML/CFT policies
2. Risk assessment methodology
3. Customer acceptance policies
4. Transaction monitoring procedures
5. Escalation and reporting procedures

### Governance

- Senior management oversight
- Designated compliance officer
- Independent audit function
- Staff training programs

---

## Part 8 - New Products and Technologies

### Risk Assessment

Before launching new DPT products/services:
1. Assess ML/TF risks
2. Implement appropriate controls
3. Document risk assessment
4. Obtain necessary approvals

### ECDD for New Products

Enhanced measures for products with:
- Anonymity features
- Non-face-to-face relationships
- Complex structures
- Cross-border elements
