# Singapore Red Flag Indicators for Digital Payment Token Service Providers

**Purpose**: Red flag indicators for identifying suspicious ML/TF activities in DPT transactions
**Key Assumptions**: Applies to all DPT licensees under Payment Services Act 2019
**Usage Patterns**: Transaction monitoring; suspicious activity detection; STR filing triggers

---

## Overview

- **Authority**: Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office (STRO), Singapore Police Force
- **Legal Basis**: Corruption, Drug Trafficking and Other Serious Crimes Act (CDSA); Terrorism (Suppression of Financing) Act (TSOFA)
- **Applies to**: All Digital Payment Token Service Providers
- **Source**: Singapore Police Force Advisory

---

## Part 1 - General Principles

### Purpose of Red Flags

- Highlight basic indicators of potential ML/TF activity
- Aid in identifying suspicious transactions
- Must not replace common sense or judgment
- Individual indicators may be insufficient alone
- Combination of indicators may suggest suspicious activity

### Customer Verification

- Check plausibility of customer declarations
- Proceed with caution for reluctant customers
- Verify information that appears false or misleading
- Be alert to difficult-to-verify information

## Part 2 - Transaction Red Flags

### Transactions Without Economic Sense

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Inconsistent activity** | Transactions that cannot be reconciled with usual customer activities |
| **Multiple accounts** | Large number of accounts with same provider and frequent inter-account transfers |
| **Structuring** | Multiple small transactions appearing to avoid reporting thresholds |
| **Rapid movement** | Funds moved quickly through accounts with no apparent business purpose |

### Unusual Transaction Patterns

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Round amounts** | Repeated transactions in exact round amounts |
| **Threshold avoidance** | Transactions just below reporting thresholds |
| **Immediate withdrawal** | Funds withdrawn immediately after deposit |
| **Third-party payments** | Unexplained payments by unrelated third parties |

### High-Value Transactions

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Inconsistent with profile** | Large transactions inconsistent with customer's financial background |
| **Unexplained source** | Unable to explain source of funds for large transactions |
| **Cash-intensive** | Large cash deposits followed by DPT purchases |

## Part 3 - Customer Behavior Red Flags

### Identity and Documentation

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fraudulent documents** | Fake, forged, or suspicious identity documents |
| **Reluctance to provide info** | Customer unwilling to provide normal required information |
| **Minimal information** | Customer provides only minimum required information |
| **False/misleading info** | Information that appears false or misleading |
| **Difficult verification** | Information difficult or expensive to verify |

### Behavioral Patterns

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Unusual urgency** | Insistence on immediate transaction completion |
| **Avoidance of records** | Attempts to avoid documentation or record-keeping |
| **Third-party control** | Appears to be acting on behalf of undisclosed third party |
| **Inconsistent knowledge** | Limited knowledge of their own transactions |

### Source of Funds/Wealth

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Unclear source** | Cannot determine source of wealth or funds |
| **Inconsistent with occupation** | Transaction values inconsistent with stated occupation |
| **Unexplained wealth** | Wealth level inconsistent with known background |

## Part 4 - Geographic Risk Indicators

### High-Risk Jurisdictions

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **FATF high-risk** | Transactions involving FATF-listed high-risk jurisdictions |
| **Sanctions nexus** | Transactions with sanctioned countries or persons |
| **Tax havens** | Unexplained transactions through known tax haven jurisdictions |
| **Conflict zones** | Transactions involving conflict or terrorism-prone regions |

### Cross-Border Patterns

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Unjustified movement** | Funds moved across borders without legitimate business purpose |
| **Circular transactions** | Funds return to origin through multiple jurisdictions |
| **No local presence** | Customer has no apparent connection to Singapore |

## Part 5 - DPT-Specific Red Flags

### Wallet and Address Indicators

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Sanctioned addresses** | Transactions with addresses on sanctions lists |
| **Mixer/tumbler use** | Funds processed through mixing services |
| **Privacy coins** | Conversion to/from privacy-enhanced cryptocurrencies |
| **Darknet association** | Addresses associated with darknet marketplaces |

### Transaction Patterns

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Chain hopping** | Rapid conversion between multiple DPT types |
| **Layering** | Complex series of transactions to obscure origin |
| **Unhosted wallets** | Heavy reliance on unhosted/self-custody wallets |
| **Rapid liquidation** | Immediate conversion of DPT to fiat currency |

### Platform and Service Indicators

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Unlicensed exchanges** | Transactions through unlicensed or high-risk exchanges |
| **P2P platforms** | Heavy use of peer-to-peer trading platforms |
| **DeFi protocols** | Unexplained interactions with decentralized protocols |

## Part 6 - Reporting Requirements

### STR Filing Obligation

When any person knows or has reasonable grounds to suspect that property may be connected to criminal activity:

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Authority** | Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office (STRO) |
| **Legal basis** | Section 45, CDSA |
| **Terrorism financing** | Sections 8 and 10, TSOFA |
| **Penalty for non-filing** | Criminal offense |

### Filing Process

1. Document the suspicious indicators
2. Gather relevant transaction and customer information
3. Submit STR through STRO portal
4. Maintain confidentiality (no tipping off)
5. Retain records for regulatory period
