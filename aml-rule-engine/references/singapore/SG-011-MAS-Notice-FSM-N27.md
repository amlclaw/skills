# MAS Notice FSM-N27: Prevention of Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism

**Source**: Monetary Authority of Singapore (mas.gov.sg)
**Published**: 30 May 2025
**Effective**: 30 June 2025
**Applies to**: Digital Token Service Providers (DTSPs)

---

## 1. Introduction

This Notice is issued under section 16 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 (FSM Act) and applies to holders of a Digital Token Service Provider licence granted under section 138 of the FSM Act.

---

## 2. Scope

### Covered Entities
- Licensed Digital Token Service Providers (DTSPs)
- Entities providing digital token services from Singapore
- Both local and offshore-focused operations

### Covered Activities
- Digital token dealing
- Digital token exchange
- Digital token custody
- Digital token transfer facilitation

---

## 3. Key Requirements

### 3.1 AML/CFT Framework

DTSPs must establish and maintain:
- AML/CFT policies and procedures
- Risk assessment framework
- Compliance management arrangements
- Independent audit function

### 3.2 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

**Standard CDD**:
- Customer identification and verification
- Beneficial owner identification
- Purpose and nature of business relationship
- Ongoing monitoring

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** required for:
- High-risk customers
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex or unusual transactions

**Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)** permitted for:
- Low-risk customers (with documented risk assessment)
- Specific low-risk products/services

### 3.3 Travel Rule (Wire Transfer Requirements)

For digital token transfers, DTSPs must:

**Originator Information Required**:
- Name
- Account number (wallet address)
- Physical address, national ID, or date/place of birth

**Beneficiary Information Required**:
- Name
- Account number (wallet address)

**Thresholds**:
- All transfers: Basic originator/beneficiary info
- Transfers â‰¥ SGD 1,500: Full information required

### 3.4 Transaction Monitoring

- Real-time monitoring systems
- Risk-based transaction screening
- Sanctions screening (OFAC, UN, Singapore MAS lists)
- Pattern detection for suspicious activities

### 3.5 Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)

- Report to Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office (STRO)
- No threshold for reporting
- Timely filing required
- Record retention: 5 years minimum

### 3.6 Record Keeping

Retain for minimum 5 years:
- CDD records
- Transaction records
- Internal reports
- Correspondence with authorities

---

## 4. Risk Assessment

DTSPs must conduct:
- Enterprise-wide ML/TF risk assessment
- Customer risk assessment
- Product/service risk assessment
- Geographic risk assessment
- Delivery channel risk assessment

---

## 5. Governance

### Board and Senior Management
- Approve AML/CFT policies
- Allocate adequate resources
- Ensure compliance culture
- Regular reporting on AML/CFT matters

### Compliance Officer
- Appoint dedicated AML/CFT compliance officer
- Direct reporting line to senior management
- Adequate authority and resources

---

## 6. Training

- AML/CFT training for all relevant staff
- Role-specific training programs
- Regular refresher training
- Documentation of training records

---

## 7. Penalties

Non-compliance may result in:
- Regulatory action by MAS
- Financial penalties
- License suspension or revocation
- Criminal prosecution
